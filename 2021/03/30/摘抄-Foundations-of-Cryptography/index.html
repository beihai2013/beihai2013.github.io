1.1.4 Fault-Tolerant Protocols and Zero-Knowledge Proofs

To illustrate the role of zero-knowledge proofs, consider a setting in which a party, called Alice, upon receiving an encrypted message from Bob, is to send Carol the least significant bit of the message. Clearly, if Alice sends only the (least significant) bit (of the message), then there is no way for Carol to know Alice did not cheat. Alice could prove that she did not cheat by revealing to Carol the entire message as well as its decryption key, but that would yield information far beyond what had been required. A much better idea is to let Alice augment the bit she sends Carol with a zero-knowledge proof that this bit is indeed the least significant bit of the message

举例说明零知识证明。假设一个参与方Alice，收到Bob的加密信息，并将信息的签名发送给Carol。如果Alice只发送签名的最后一位给Carol，Carol是无法知道Alice是否作弊的。Alice可以通过发送给Carol全部信息和解密密钥以证明自己没有作弊，但是这会导致信息泄露。一个更好的方案是，Alice使用零知识证明自己发送的确实是签名的最后一位。
